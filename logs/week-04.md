## Goals
- Begin implementation for the synthetic quantum reliability dataset.
- Create initial quantum circuits based on related work.
- Inject errors into circuits and compare outputs against baseline results.

## Approach and Implementation

This week, I began moving from literature review into implementation. Based on the related work I had reviewed, I started creating initial quantum circuits for the project and testing how the circuits behave under different error conditions. The goal was to begin building a synthetic benchmark that compares clean circuit execution against noisy circuit execution.

I created initial circuits and began injecting different types of errors. I also started recording outputs so that the results from noisy executions could be compared against the baseline outputs from clean executions. This helped establish the first version of the implementation pipeline for generating and evaluating noisy quantum circuit behavior.

During my meeting with Dr. Asadinia, I showed her my progress. She recommended that I expand the evaluation by adding more metrics based on the related work I had reviewed. She also told me to add more circuits, but emphasized that the circuits should not be randomly invented. Instead, they should be based on circuits used in related work. 

Dr. Asadinia also recommended that I explore whether the circuits can be run on real quantum hardware. 

## Results

- Began implementation for synthetic circuit execution and noisy circuit generation.
- Created initial quantum circuits for testing.
- Began comparing clean execution results against noisy execution results.
- Received feedback from Dr. Asadinia to expand the metrics, features, and circuit set.
